The Environment

10% of global CO₂ emissions come from the fashion industry.
And yet — 40% of all clothing produced is never sold.
It is burned. Landfilled. Destroyed.

Sources: United Nations Environment Programme — Fashion emissions (~10% of global CO₂): unep.org

European Environment Agency — Textile waste, overproduction, environmental impact in Europe: eea.europa.eu

Ellen MacArthur Foundation — ~40% of clothing unsold / underused & systemic waste: ellenmacarthurfoundation.org

This is not something that just happens.
The current system requires overproduction.

New legislation challenges this problem head-on.
From July 2026, the EU requires brands to take responsibility for unsold inventory.

Sources: Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation — Includes ban on destruction of unsold textiles (coming into force mid-decade): environment.ec.europa.eu

EU Strategy for Sustainable and Circular Textiles: environment.ec.europa.eu

The industry knows what the problem is.
It doesn't yet have a solution.

SELFWEAR abolishes the problem at its root

There is no unsold stock.

Sold first.

Statista — E-commerce return rates in fashion: statista.com

Narvar — 60–70% returns due to fit issues: narvar.com

SELFWEAR eliminates this.
